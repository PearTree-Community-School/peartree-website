## Mirrors, Myths, and The Sacred Ordinary

**Date:** September 9, 2025
**URL:** https://medium.com/@thechildrenarealwaysours.com/mirrors-myths-and-the-sacred-ordinary-5f744b506064

Dear Families,

This week I have sat with so many incredible parents. Parents who are so hard on themselves, holding themselves to impossible standards. Parents who are feeling heavy, carrying injury, illness, or loss. Parents juggling a new baby. Parents who are navigating financial challenges or deep stress. Parents who are over-apologetic for running late, or crying at drop-off because their little one is crying too. This is a love letter to them, and to you, because you deserve to be held with the same compassion that you pour into your children.

In these first weeks of school, many families are still carrying the weight of transition. You may find yourself running late, rushing through drop-off, leaving a child who is still teary, or picking up after a day that feels too short to hold everything. Maybe you door-dash dinner instead of cooking. Maybe you've eaten leftovers for three days. Maybe you argued in front of your child. Maybe you completely lost your temper and raised your voice to an octave you swore you never would. Maybe you were impatient when your child took too long to choose an outfit or put on their shoes. Perhaps your three-year-old needed more time than you had to give them, and you raised your voice out of sheer exhaustion and the disappointment you felt in yourself for not being able to provide what they needed. Maybe you feel heavy with sadness and fear about the world they are growing up in. All of these moments live in the ordinary rhythm of family life. And yet, it is here, in the **sacred ordinary,** that the deepest truths are revealed.

In the **MIRROR Framework**, the first principle is **Mirroring**. Children learn who they are not only through what we say about them, but through what they see reflected in our eyes, our words, our tones, our gestures.

## The Children Are Always Ours: Adaptive Mirroring and the Art of Shining Safely

**Date:** November 2, 2025
**Publication:** Women Write
**URL:** https://medium.com/women-write/the-children-are-always-ours-adaptive-mirroring-and-the-art-of-shining-safely-7fd9fda2536a

When my son was a boy, I did everything I could to make sure he knew his voice mattered. I brought him to the trainings I led and gave him real parts to present — little rules to deliver, ideas to explain, moments where adults were asked to listen to him as an equal. I told him to walk the room, to project, to look people in the eye. I wanted him to understand that brilliance belonged to him; that he could speak truth to power, and that even as a child, his words carried weight.

When he was fourteen, that confidence met the world. Late one night, he and a few friends were outside our townhouse doing parkour — climbing the terrace walls, laughing, burning off energy. I knew they were there; I'd brought them snacks earlier. When I heard him yell my name, I ran outside to see police dragging him by the handcuffs, scraping his whole body against the cement. He kept repeating, *I live here. I live here.*
