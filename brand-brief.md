## 2. MISSION, PHILOSOPHY & DIFFERENTIATORS

### Core Mission
A social justice-informed Montessori school serving historically marginalized communities through academically rigorous, socio-emotionally informed, civically engaged, and arts-infused education.

### Educational Philosophy
1. **Montessori Foundation** — Traditional Montessori curriculum (Practical Life, Sensory, Cultural Studies, Geography, Mathematics, Language Arts)
2. **Social Justice Lens** — Decolonized curriculum centering Black, Indigenous, and students of color
3. **Nature-Based Learning** — Hands-on sensorial experience with the natural world
4. **Play-Based Learning** — Purposeful play integrated alongside structured Montessori work
5. **Project-Based Learning** — Design theory, problem-posing, problem-solving
6. **Mixed-Age Classrooms** — Intentional social-emotional curriculum developing empathy and community

### What Makes Pear Tree Unique (Key Selling Points)
1. **Peer-reviewed recognition** — Featured in the London Review of Education for decolonial curriculum approaches
2. **Equity commitment** — 80% financial aid rate; no family turned away for inability to pay
3. **BIPOC-centered** — 95% students of color, intentional BIPOC educator hiring
4. **Retention** — "Our very first students are still with us" (preschool through elementary pipeline)
5. **Community-driven** — Walk-a-thons, Black Heritage Month Showcase, parent volunteer culture, potlucks
6. **Research-backed methodology** — Blended Montessori + nature + play frameworks with mapped scope and sequence
7. **Part of a movement** — Connected to Montessori for Social Justice (est. 2013)
8. **Holistic development** — Social-emotional, academic, artistic, civic engagement

## 3. MICHELE HAMILTON — FOUNDER PROFILE

### Biography
Michele Hamilton is a trailblazing educator with **27+ years of experience** in transformative educational change. She grew up in a low-income household in **East Oakland** and navigated the Oakland Unified School District despite early academic struggles — an experience that fueled her lifelong commitment to educational equity and liberation.

She founded Pear Tree Community School as both a model school and a vital training/observation site for educational policy development. She later established **Seeds to Roots Oakland**, the school's sister organization and training arm, to scale the Pear Tree model nationally.

### The MIRROR Framework
Hamilton created the **MIRROR framework**, a proprietary approach to supporting BIPOC student success:

- **M — Mirroring**: Immerses children in environments that provide mirroring and reflection, building deep self-identity
- **I — Identification**: Connects students to their heritage, presenting their ancestors as innovators
- **R — Reclamation**: Recovers narratives often distorted or erased, empowering students with accurate identity and history
- **R — Relationships**: Builds culturally responsive connections, creating a supportive environment
- **O — Orientation**: Aligns school systems, policies, and practices with the mission of centering BIPOC students
- **R — Reflection**: Encourages ongoing self-assessment and research-driven development for equitable, liberating environments

### The Pear Tree Framework (PTF)
A replicable educational model designed to be scaled nationally. The framework:
- Combines Montessori methods with social justice curriculum rooted in BIPOC cultures
- Uses trauma-informed practices that understand the impact of historical trauma
- Helps children think deeply and critically, pose questions, and solve problems
- Employs anti-bias hiring practices and social justice curriculum development

### Seeds to Roots Oakland (Training Arm)
- **Website:** [seedstorootsoakland.com](https://www.seedstorootsoakland.com/)
- **Mission:** Revolutionize education by fostering equity and liberation
- **Audience:** Education leaders, founders, superintendents, teachers, policymakers
- **Method:** Educators observe the Pear Tree Framework in real-time at the school (observation site)
- **Training techniques:** Interactive activities, visual arts, theater of the oppressed techniques
- Trains educators who may not hold a Montessori credential to implement these methods

### Key Outcome Statistic
> **While only 22% of Black and Latino students nationally reach proficiency in literacy, 90% of Pear Tree students exceed benchmarks.**

### Recognition & Media
- **London Review of Education** — Peer-reviewed article on Pear Tree's decolonial curriculum (2022)

## 4. STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS FOR NEW WEBSITE

### Primary Audiences
1. **Prospective Families** — Parents seeking values-aligned, rigorous education for their children
2. **Donors & Philanthropists** — Individuals and foundations aligned with educational equity
3. **Community Partners** — Local organizations, businesses, and advocates

### Recommended Site Architecture

```
/                       → Hero + Mission + Call to Action
/about                  → Story, Mission, Vision, Values, History
/about/team             → Staff bios with photos (BIPOC educators highlighted)
/about/social-justice   → Decolonial approach, Montessori for Social Justice
/programs/preschool     → Ages 2+, curriculum, daily schedule
/programs/elementary    → TK–5, classrooms, project-based learning
/programs/summer-camp   → Seasonal program
/admissions             → Application process, tours, financial aid
/admissions/financial-aid → 80% aid rate, equity commitment, sliding scale
/community              → Events, parent involvement, volunteer info
/community/news         → Blog/news feed (replaces hidden ParentSquare content)
/impact                 → Outcomes, testimonials, research recognition
/support                → Donation page, sponsorship, Walk-a-Thon, wish lists
/support/volunteer      → Volunteer opportunities
/contact                → Form, map, phone, email
```

### Content Strategy Priorities

**For Student Recruitment:**
- Lead with the "why" — social justice mission, not just Montessori method
- Surface parent testimonials and student stories
- Show the community (events, potlucks, showcases) — this is a massive differentiator
- Highlight the preschool-to-5th-grade pipeline ("Our very first students are still with us")
- Feature the classroom names (Butterfly, Dragonfly, etc.) — they create identity and belonging

**For Donor Recruitment:**
- Create a dedicated "/support" or "/give" page with clear giving tiers
- Lead with the 80% financial aid statistic — this is the most compelling donor argument
- Feature the London Review of Education recognition as academic credibility
- Show the "no family turned away" policy as proof of mission alignment
- Publish an annual impact report (even a simple one-page version)
- Integrate with a donation platform (e.g., Donorbox, GoFundMe, or similar)

**For SEO & Discovery:**
- Target keywords: "social justice school Oakland," "Montessori Oakland," "Black Montessori school," "diverse preschool Oakland," "Rockridge school"
- Publish the blog content that currently only lives on ParentSquare
- Add structured data (School schema markup)

### Brand Voice Guidelines
- **Warm but serious** — this is joyful education with deep purpose
- **Community-centered** — "we" language, collective achievement
- **Justice-forward** — unapologetic about centering BIPOC students
- **Evidence-based** — cite the research, name the pedagogy
- **Invitational** — "Join us," "Be part of," not "Apply now"
- **Anti-corporate** — avoid sterile education-industry language; this is a community school

### Visual Direction
- Photography-heavy: real students, real classrooms, real events
- Earth tones + bold accents (aligned with nature-based philosophy)
- Hand-drawn or organic design elements (not slick corporate)
- The pear tree itself as a symbol: rooted, growing, bearing fruit
