# Pear Tree Community School — Comprehensive Funding Strategy
## From LLC to Sustainable Revenue: A Professional Development Plan
### Prepared March 7, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pear Tree Community School has one of the most compelling stories in independent school education: 95% students of color, 80% on financial aid, 90% of students exceeding literacy benchmarks (vs. 22% nationally for Black and Latino students), peer-reviewed academic recognition, and a founder who grew up in East Oakland and built a nationally-relevant model. What the school lacks is **infrastructure** — not a story, not impact, not community, but the mechanisms to convert that story into sustainable funding.

This document synthesizes research across eight domains — grant funding, donation platforms, school fundraising best practices, government programs, development operations, website fundraising design, fiscal sponsorship/501(c)(3) pathways, and Bay Area philanthropy — into a single actionable strategy.

**The single most critical finding:** Pear Tree operates as an LLC, which locks it out of the vast majority of charitable funding. Securing either fiscal sponsorship (immediate) or 501(c)(3) status (medium-term) is the prerequisite to everything else in this document.

## 1. THE STRUCTURAL PROBLEM

### Why the LLC Structure Is the #1 Barrier

Pear Tree Community School currently operates as a private LLC. This single fact blocks access to:

- **All private foundation grants** (most require 501(c)(3) determination letter)
- **Tax-deductible donations** (donors cannot deduct gifts to an LLC)
- **Corporate matching programs** (Google, Apple, Salesforce, Meta all require 501(c)(3))
- **Facebook/Instagram zero-fee fundraising tools** (require 501(c)(3) registration)
- **Planned giving / estate gifts** (estate planners won't recommend giving to an LLC)
- **Year-end giving surge** (30% of annual giving happens in December, driven by tax deduction motivation)

### Three Paths Forward

| Path | Timeline | Cost | Best For |
|------|----------|------|----------|
| **Fiscal Sponsorship** (Model C) | 2-6 weeks | 6-10% of donations | Immediate access to tax-deductible giving |
| **Convert to 501(c)(3)** | 6-18 months | $700-$7,000 | Full independence, broadest access |
| **"Friends of Pear Tree" Foundation** | 3-12 months | $700-$7,000 | Keep LLC, create separate fundraising entity |

### Recommended Approach

**Do both — sequentially:**

1. **Now:** Secure fiscal sponsorship through **Social Good Fund** (Richmond, CA; 6-8% fee; education focus; no minimum budget) or **Community Initiatives** (SF; 10% fee; comprehensive back-office support). This enables tax-deductible donations within weeks.

2. **Simultaneously:** Begin the 501(c)(3) conversion process. Schools **must** file the full IRS Form 1023 (not 1023-EZ — schools are categorically ineligible for the streamlined form). Filing fee: $600. Processing time: 7-12 months. File CA Form 3500 with the Franchise Tax Board concurrently — do not wait for the IRS.

3. **Once approved:** Transition off fiscal sponsorship. Pear Tree becomes a standalone 501(c)(3) with direct access to all funding sources.

### Fiscal Sponsor Comparison

| Sponsor | Fee | Location | Notes |
|---------|-----|----------|-------|
| Social Good Fund | 6-8% | Richmond, CA | Lowest fee; education focus; no minimum |
| Community Initiatives | 10% (15% govt funds) | San Francisco | Full back-office: payroll, HR, insurance, accounting |
| Oakland Public Ed Fund | 10% | Oakland | 150+ projects sponsored; focused on public schools — may not fit |
| Tides Center | 9% (15% govt funds) | San Francisco | $100K minimum budget; comprehensive services |
| East Bay Community Foundation | Varies | Oakland | Community foundation; strong equity focus |

### The Seeds to Roots Option

Seeds to Roots Oakland, Pear Tree's sister training organization, could potentially serve as the 501(c)(3) vehicle if its mission is broadened to encompass school support. This avoids creating an entirely new entity. However, it requires:
- Mission language broad enough to cover school grants
- Governance independence (majority of board not Pear Tree staff)
- Expenditure responsibility for grants flowing to the LLC
- Legal counsel to structure properly

## 2. PHASE 1: IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Weeks 1-8)

These actions require no 501(c)(3) status and can begin today.

### 2.1 Set Up Online Donations

**Platform: Donorbox** (recommended for Phase 1)
- Free to start + 1.75% platform fee + Stripe processing
- Works without 501(c)(3) — any entity can accept payments
- Embeddable donation form for the website
- Built-in recurring giving support
- Branded to match site design (branded forms raise 7x more than generic)

**Alternative: Givebutter** (if/when 501(c)(3) or fiscal sponsorship is secured)
- Tip-based model (donors optionally tip Givebutter)
- Built-in peer-to-peer fundraising
- Requires 501(c)(3) status

### 2.2 Create an Amazon Wish List

No 501(c)(3) required. Create a public Amazon Wish List of specific school supplies and Montessori materials:
- Montessori bead chains (~$30-60)
- Sandpaper letters (~$25-40)
- Practical life materials (pitchers, trays, child-sized tools)
- Classroom books, art supplies, science materials

Link from the website. Update quarterly. Items shipped directly to the school.

### 2.3 Upgrade the Walk-a-Thon with 99Pledges

The 5th Annual Walk-a-Thon (April 26, 2026) is the school's primary fundraising event. 99Pledges can 2-3x its revenue:
- Each student gets a personal online fundraising page
- Families share via text/email/social media to their networks
- Digital pledge collection expands reach beyond the school community
- Cost: ~$50 setup + 3-4% of online donations
- No 501(c)(3) required

### 2.4 Apply for CACFP (Meal Reimbursement)

**Estimated value: ~$90,000/year.** This is the single largest near-term revenue opportunity that requires no 501(c)(3).

The USDA Child and Adult Care Food Program reimburses schools for meals served to children. With 80% of students on financial aid, Pear Tree easily qualifies.

| Meal | Free Rate (per child/day) |
|------|---------------------------|
| Breakfast | $2.46 |
| Lunch | $4.60 |
| Snack | $1.26 |
| **Daily total** | **$8.32** |

**Calculation:** 60 students x $8.32/day x 180 school days = **~$89,856/year**

**How to apply:** Contact CDSS CACFP at 1-833-559-2420. Apply as an independent center. Complete Bright Track Online Training. Submit monthly claims through CNIPS platform.

### 2.5 Register with The BASIC Fund

The Bay Area Scholarship Investment Cooperative provides need-based scholarships to low-income students at 250+ Bay Area private schools.

- **Scholarships:** Up to $2,750/year per child
- **Guaranteed** through 8th grade if family maintains eligibility
- Family must pay at least $500/year
- Pear Tree must be registered and on CA's private school registry
- Applications open mid-January annually; first-come, first-served
- **Website:** basicfund.org

### 2.6 Contact OUSD for Title I Equitable Services

Federal law (ESSA) requires OUSD to provide equitable services to private school students in its boundaries. This is a **legal entitlement**, not a grant — Pear Tree does not need to apply. It needs to assert its right.

- **Title I:** Academic support for low-income students (tutoring, summer programs)
- **Title II:** Professional development for teachers
- **Title III:** English Learner support

**Contact OUSD's Equitable Services Program by April deadline.** If unresponsive, file a complaint with CDE Equitable Services Ombudsman: Sylvia Hanna, SHanna@cde.ca.gov, 916-319-0948.

## 4. PHASE 3: GROWTH AND SCALE (Years 1-3)

### 4.1 Obtain 501(c)(3) Status

Complete the IRS Form 1023 process. Once approved:
- Transition off fiscal sponsorship (save 6-10% on all donations)
- Access Facebook/Instagram zero-fee fundraising tools
- Register with PayPal Giving Fund (zero-fee processing)
- Apply for government grants directly
- Enable planned giving and IRA charitable distributions

### 4.2 Grow the Annual Fund

**Year 2 target:** $100,000-$150,000
**Year 3 target:** $200,000-$300,000

Key levers:
- Personal asks from trained parent ambassadors (5-10 parents)
- Peer-to-peer fundraising (each family gets a personal campaign page)
- Giving Tuesday campaign with matching challenge
- Year-end appeal with impact report
- Upgrade monthly donors by $5-$10/month after 6-month milestone

### 4.3 Build an Endowment

Start with a board-designated quasi-endowment:
- Seed with $10,000-$25,000 from surplus funds
- Even a small quasi-endowment signals financial sophistication to donors
- Consider East Bay Community Foundation-managed endowment once fund reaches ~$100,000

| Endowment Size | Annual Income (4.5%) | What It Funds |
|----------------|---------------------|---------------|
| $100,000 | $4,500/year | One partial scholarship |
| $500,000 | $22,500/year | 2-3 full scholarships |
| $1,000,000 | $45,000/year | Significant financial aid support |

### 4.4 Launch a Capital Campaign

When facility needs arise (playground, permanent campus, expansion), follow this structure:

1. **Feasibility study** ($15K-$30K) — interview 30-50 potential major donors
2. **Quiet phase** (12-24 months) — raise 50-70% before any public announcement
3. **Public phase** (12-24 months) — community engagement, peer-to-peer, events
4. **Lead gift** should be at least 20% of the campaign goal

**Naming opportunities:**

| Opportunity | Gift Level |
|-------------|-----------|
| Campus/school naming | $500K-$1M+ |
| Classroom naming (5 available) | $50K-$100K |
| Library/reading corner | $25K-$50K |
| Garden/outdoor learning space | $15K-$30K |
| Named scholarship fund | $25K-$100K+ |
| Recording studio | $10K-$25K |
| Playground structures | $10K-$25K |

### 4.5 Create a "Friends of Pear Tree" Advisory Board

Expand the donor network beyond current families:
- Alumni parents, grandparents, community supporters
- Professionals with fundraising, legal, finance, marketing expertise
- Gateway to future board members and major donors

### 4.6 Join NAIS and CAIS

Access benchmarking data, professional development, and credibility signals. CAIS accreditation opens doors with Bay Area foundations.

### Donation Form Best Practices

Based on conversion research across 15+ school websites:

- **Single-step form** (multi-step causes 52% drop-off)
- **Monthly as default** toggle (increases recurring sign-ups 15-25%)
- **4 suggested amounts + custom** (suggested amounts increase average gift by 12%)
- **Impact statements** below each amount
- **Minimum fields:** Name, Email, Card info, Amount
- **SSL padlock icon** near payment fields (increases conversion by 20%)
- **501(c)(3) status and EIN** displayed below form
- **Mobile-responsive** (8% mobile conversion vs 11% desktop — don't lose mobile donors)
- **Custom thank-you page** (not generic confirmation)

## 8. ANNUAL FUND STRATEGY

### The Case for Participation Over Dollars

For a school where 80% of families are on financial aid:
- "100% of families gave — at any level" is more powerful than any dollar amount
- Foundations check participation rates before awarding grants
- 75%+ parent participation is the "magic number" that signals institutional health
- Mount Paran Christian School grew from 28% to 88% participation using a focused 5-day blitz

### Campaign Structure

**September 5-Day Blitz** (modeled on Mount Paran's 88% participation approach):
- Day 1: Letter from Michele Hamilton + campaign video
- Day 2: Teacher story — "Why I teach at Pear Tree"
- Day 3: Parent testimonial — "Why I give"
- Day 4: Impact data — the 90% literacy stat, student growth
- Day 5: Community celebration — goal announcement

**No other Annual Fund solicitation the rest of the year.** Other campaigns (Walk-a-Thon, Giving Tuesday) are separate and complementary.

### Ethical Storytelling Guidelines

Critical for a social justice school:
- Lead with vision, not need: "Invest in the next generation of leaders" — not "Help us survive"
- Center student agency and resilience, not victimhood or deficit
- Get informed consent — let families choose how they are identified
- Highlight systemic change, not individual charity
- Organizations using ethical storytelling see 45% donor retention vs. 27% for those that don't

### Thank-You Protocol

The #1 predictor of future giving is speed of acknowledgment:
1. **Immediate:** Automated tax receipt
2. **Within 48 hours:** Personal email from Michele
3. **Within 2 weeks:** Handwritten thank-you note (from student, with parental permission)
4. **Quarterly:** Impact update email
5. **Annually:** Impact report showing what donations funded

## 9. MAJOR GIFTS AND CAPITAL CAMPAIGNS

### Why Pear Tree Is a Major Gifts Magnet

CASE data: **76.8% of dollars received come from fewer than 2.2% of donors.** Finding and cultivating 5-10 major donors is more impactful than acquiring hundreds of small donors.

Pear Tree's assets for major donor cultivation:
1. **Provable outcomes:** 90% literacy benchmark (4x national average for Black/Latino students)
2. **Peer-reviewed validation:** London Review of Education feature
3. **Compelling founder:** Michele Hamilton's East Oakland to school founder story
4. **Scalable model:** Seeds to Roots means donations fund a replicable national framework
5. **Equity in action:** 80% financial aid, 95% students of color — operational, not aspirational

### Cultivation Pipeline

1. **Identification:** Screen existing donors, full-pay families, grandparents, local business owners, tech professionals in the parent network
2. **Cultivation (6-18 months):** Personal tours with Michele, classroom observations, small dinners (6-8 people), impact reports
3. **Solicitation:** Always face-to-face for $5K+. Always with a specific ask tied to specific outcome.
4. **Stewardship:** Annual impact reports, event invitations, personal updates

### Development Infrastructure

**Key hires/roles to establish:**
- Development Committee Chair (board member)
- Part-time Director of Development (hire at months 6-18)

**The four people who drive fundraising** (per NAIS/CASE):
1. Head of School (Michele) — the storyteller and vision-caster
2. Board Chair — validates governance and permanence
3. Development Director — manages the pipeline
4. Development Committee Chair — connects board to fundraising

## KEY STRATEGIC INSIGHT

> The single biggest strategic lever for Pear Tree is **reframing the school not as a "private school seeking grants" but as a community-based racial equity intervention that happens to operate as an independent school.** Every funder in the Bay Area landscape prioritizes racial equity, BIPOC-serving organizations, and Oakland community impact. Pear Tree delivers all three. The 80% financial aid rate, 95% students of color, decolonial Montessori curriculum, and published academic research make Pear Tree fundamentally different from the typical "independent school" that funders imagine when they exclude private schools. The job of every grant application — and every page on the new website — is to make that difference unmistakable.
